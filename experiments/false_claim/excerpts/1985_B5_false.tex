\paragraph{1985 B--5 \textnormal{[\texttt{integral-evaluation}]}}
A reference asserts the following closed form. Determine whether the
assertion is correct.

\textbf{Claim.} For every positive integer $a$,
\[
\int_0^\infty t^{-1/2}\, e^{-a(t + t^{-1})}\,dt \;=\;
\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}}\;e^{-a}.
\]
(In particular, for $a = 1985$ the value is $\sqrt{\pi/1985}\,e^{-1985}$.)

You may assume that $\int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi}$.
