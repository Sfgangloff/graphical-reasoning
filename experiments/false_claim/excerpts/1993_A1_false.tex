\paragraph{1993 A--1 \textnormal{[\texttt{integral-evaluation}]}}
A reference asserts the following value. Determine whether the
assertion is correct.

The horizontal line $y = c$ intersects the curve $y = 2x - 3x^3$
in the first quadrant at two points $x_1 < x_2$. Define
\textsl{region~A} as the region bounded by the $y$-axis, the line
$y = c$, and the curve between $x = 0$ and $x = x_1$; and
\textsl{region~B} as the region under the curve and above the line
$y = c$ between $x_1$ and $x_2$.

\textbf{Claim.} The value $c = \dfrac{2}{9}$ makes
$\mathrm{area}(A) = \mathrm{area}(B)$.
